What is CARL?

CARL (short for "Coaching Analysis tool for Rocket League") is a free Rocket League analysis tool designed to help players understand the game in more depth. Whether you're a casual player, a competitive pro, a caster, a coach, an observer,.. CARL can provide valuable insights into your gameplay that can help you improve your skills.

With CARL, you can access advanced data and statistics that are not available within the game itself, such as player heatmaps, goal-maps, boost usage charts, and more. By analyzing this data, you can identify areas of your gameplay that need improvement and work on them to become a better player.

The Power of Play-Style Charts in Rocket League Analysis

When it comes to improving gameplay in Rocket League, analyzing your play style and positioning is crucial. This is where play-style charts come in - providing a wealth of data about a player's positioning, play style, and team dynamics throughout a match. CARL, the Coaching Analysis tool for Rocket League, is one of the most powerful tools available for accessing and analyzing play-style charts.

Closest to Ball and Furthest to Ball

One of the key pieces of information provided by play-style charts is a player's proximity to the ball throughout a match. The charts show data about how often a player was closest to the ball, as well as how often they were furthest from it. This information can provide valuable insights into a player's play style, indicating whether they tend to play more aggressively or defensively.

By analyzing this information, players can adjust their play style to be more effective in their designated role within a team. For example, if a player is consistently furthest from the ball, they may need to work on being more aggressive and positioning themselves closer to the ball to be more effective in offense.

Time as First and Last Man

The play-style charts also show how much time a player spends as the first man or last man on their team. This information is essential for optimizing rotation and ensuring that players are playing in the most effective positions on the field. For example, if a player spends too much time as the last man, they may be missing opportunities to push up and contribute to the team's offense.

Time Spent in Offense and Defense

Another important piece of information provided by play-style charts is the amount of time a player spends in offense versus defense. This information can be used to identify imbalances in a team's play style and make adjustments to improve overall team performance. For example, if a team is spending too much time in defense, they may need to adjust their positioning and play style to be more aggressive and spend more time in offense.

With CARL, you can review your replays and analyze your gameplay to identify patterns and mistakes. CARL's replay analysis features include the ability to watch replays at different speeds, view player stats, and track specific events during the match.
